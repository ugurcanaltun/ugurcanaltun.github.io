Uğur Can Altun

I am an M.Sc. student in Computer Engineering at Bilkent University, advised by Assoc. Prof. Dr. Hamdi Dibeklioğlu. Before shifting my focus to multimodal affective computing, I was an undergraduate researcher in the Bilkent University Software Engineering and Data Analytics Research Group (BILSEN), under the supervision of Assoc. Prof. Dr. Eray Tüzün. I was also a visiting undergraduate researcher in the ZEST group at the University of Zurich, where I worked under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Alberto Bacchelli. In addition, I worked as a software engineer at Mercedes-Benz Tech.

My research focuses on multimodal affective computing, speech and audio processing, and audio-language models, with an emphasis on developing deep learning models that infer human affect, linguistic content, and mental states from speech. I am particularly interested in capturing the nuanced characteristics of speech to better understand speakers’ emotions, personality traits, and clinical conditions, as well as in developing assistive speech technologies based on speech analysis and generation. My recent work explores an audio-language framework for depression severity estimation from clinical interview speech.

Severity Assessment of Depression through Audio-Language Multiple Instance Learning with Affect- and Semantic-Aware Fusion
Uğur Can Altun, Hamdi Dibeklioğlu
Completed manuscript; targeting IEEE Transactions on Affective Computing, 2026

An audio-language framework that frames depression severity estimation from clinical-interview speech as a multiple instance learning problem and fuses affect- and semantic-aware representations. Reaches state-of-the-art accuracy on the E-DAIC and DAIC benchmarks.
